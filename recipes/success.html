Recipe for Success

Description

I love nothing more than a recipe with a family history, and this one is no exception. My grandparents used to make it back in the old country (they were very progressive for their time, and both my grandmother and my grandfather liked to work on successful projects and then enjoy a nice warm bowl of success when they were done). This is one of the oldest recipes in the book, and there are as many ways to do it as there are people in the world. But it just tastes better when you know where it came from, you know? So please, enjoy some good old fashioned success.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 to 2 cups confidence
  • an equal measure of humility
  • 3 tablespoons of patience
  • 2 teaspoons ambition
  • 2 teaspoons creativity
  • sense of humor, to taste

Steps

  1. Over medium low heat, bring your confidence to a simmer. It will take some time to come up to temperature, but the slower this part goes, the better the result will be. Meanwhile, daydream about what you want to achieve.
  2. Once you're good and psyched up, you need to take yourself down a peg. Add your humility to the pan and turn up heat to high. This part will hurt and smell like burning â€” don't worry, it's supposed to do that. Remember that you're a beginner and you have no idea what you're doing. Then, when you start to feel a lot of doubt, proceed to Step 3.
  3. Crush up patience into a fine, spreadable paste. Add some of it to the pan to help you tolerate feeling like a fraud and a scaredy-cat and set the rest aside for later.
  4. Keep cooking everything while you make a plan to work on your project every day and other plans to hold yourself to it. Keep tasting, and add humor as necessary, especially if it ever starts to taste like frustration or rage.
  5. Mix creativity into your patience paste and stir it slowly into the pan. You want it to blend in smoothly so that there are no clumps and it's evenly distributed into the confidence and humility mix. You can ask anyone who tastes the finished to guess the secret ingredient.
