Recipe for Mediocrity

Description

This recipe is perfect for a weeknight dinner, no fuss no musss. Not everything has to be perfect, or crappy, sometimes you just need something to be fine.

Ingredients

  • Dried pasta, any shape
  • One jar of tomato sauce, flavored with basil or garlic if you're feeling fancy
  • Parmesan cheese, a bunch
  • Butter, a stick at most
  • Salt

Steps

  1. Salt a big pot of water and bring it to a boil
  2. Dump some of the red sauce into a small pot and set it over low heat. Ignore it for a while. Put some olive oil in if you're really motivated.
  3. When the water comes to a boil, grab a few handfuls of pasta and throw them in. Stir, then look at the box to see how long to cook. Set a timer for that amount of time.
  4. Meanwhile, grate your parmesan cheese if you bought it in a block. Otherwise, if you bought it pre-grated, just dump out a bunch onto a plate. Set aside.
  5. When the timer goes off, drain your pasta.
  6. Put the big pot back on the stove over low heat and melt some butter in it. My personal opinion is there can never be too much, but use your judgment.
  7. Put pasta back into big pot with melted butter, pour on warmed up red sauce, and stir in a giant pile of parmesan.
  8. Eat a totally normal dinner!
